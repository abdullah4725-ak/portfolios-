Process

How I actually work.

The happy path is easy. I build for everything else.

01

Map the failure points first

Before a single node gets written, I document what happens when the data is wrong, when the AI returns garbage, when the approver is on leave. Most builders skip this. It's why their systems break silently at 2am.

02

Build a decision engine, not a workflow

A Zap that fires when a form is filled is a workflow. What I build is a system with a rulebook, an escalation protocol, and a decision framework it runs on every interaction. The difference matters when things go sideways.

03

Hard rules, not soft prompts

I do not write "please be careful" into a prompt and call it guardrails. The system is structurally blocked from guessing when confidence drops below threshold. The AI stops and escalates. That's architecture, not instruction.

04

Ship with an audit trail and a kill switch

Every system I deliver logs what it did and why. If something needs a human, a human gets notified. If the stakes are high enough, there's a kill switch. You do not have to watch it run.
